You are handed the keys to government and a cast of thousands to staff it with. Take whoever fortune deals you, choose their seat, and fill all twelve seats of the cabinet. Then hold a general election — fought the way Britain really fights them, one constituency at a time. Win a majority and you govern; fall short and you lead the opposition. The dream is the impossible one: every seat in the House.
